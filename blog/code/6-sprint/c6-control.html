When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

When I feel I have tried a hundred different ways to fix something/ figure out how to approach a problem I tend to start feeling a bit burnt out and like tearing my hair out. Not a great feeling. Though I know it is absurd and I should feel this way I generally feel a bit reticent to ask for help as I love the feeling of figuring things out for myself. I have pushed that feeling aside however and do ask for help which has been really fantastic. I support the disproportiate response comes when I feel like something should be SO simple yet I can' make heads or tales of how to approach it and make it work.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

It is a fantastic approach to getting out of the loop of failed attempts and frustration and figuring out alternate ways of solving the issue i.e. doing a simple google search or asking for help.
