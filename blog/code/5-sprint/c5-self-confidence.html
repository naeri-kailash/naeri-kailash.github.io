What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

Mindfulness is in a sense increasing how observant you are about things taking place within and outside of your self. It may bring you more in tune with your breath, your physical position and bodily reactions to internal and external stimuli and perhaps most importantly your emotional reactions to these event. If you think of the mind as something akin to a wild monkey, climbing and swinging through it trees without a care in the world, mindfulness in developing an ability to observe this monkey in all its states. This is where mindfulness becomes self awareness. Self awareness is an incredibly important and undervalued skill that can vastly change an individuals life and wellbeing. Mindfulness could go from something as mundane as realising you are about to bite into that sumptuous piece of cake that you promised yourself you wouldn't eat, knowing why you were about to do it and perhaps even having the power to put the cake down. Or it could range to catching yourself before making unnecessary nasty comments towards someone or stopping yourself before throwing your phone to the ground in frustration. Having the self control in said situations is only a side effect of mindfulness where the primary result is self awareness - being able to see your emotions and their causes as the arise and subside.
