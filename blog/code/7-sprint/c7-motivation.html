How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

I live my life quite spontaneously, I don't have a habit of planning things too thoroughly/ if at all. In this sense it could appear that I rely on pleasure a lot for happiness but on reflection I have come to understand this is not entirely accurate. I put a great importance in listening to my conscience and not wasting time on things that will not prove beneficial for myself and others. For example I have had employment in various fields - one of which I could not stand being retail - feeling like I was trying to coerse people into buying things they did not want or need. However when working with children and adults with special needs I have felt very inspired and happy in my work environment as I felt that my energy was going into something invaluable. Helping those who really needed it. In this I found purpose. I hope to hold on to that value in future employment.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, \n What are your core values? \n What do you stand for?

Never doing things at the expense of others or the environment. For this reason I am a vegan as I do not believe it is justifiable to eat animal or fish products when it both takes lives and damages the environment. In the same way I try to live my life in a way that doesn't take advantage of or harm others, rather has a beneficial impact. This is difficult to achieve in a lot of ways considering how society is set up, and how we often do not know where products really come from but we can do our best can't we. I look forward to having a skillset I can put to use in places where I can can greater impact, for example in an NGO.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

I found it a very interesting approach as generally my meditative practise has not had a focus in that sense. It was a great way to neutralize feelings about instances of success and failure and find a comfortable a healthy middle ground.
